Project 1

Sustainable Fertilizer Production from Urine, Epsom Salt, and Wood Ash

Objective:

Develop a simple and effective method to produce a balanced and sustainable fertilizer using readily available materials: urine, Epsom salt, and wood ash.

Materials:

  • Urine: Rich in nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium.
  • Epsom Salt (Magnesium Sulfate): Provides magnesium and sulfur.
  • Wood Ash: Contains potassium and other trace minerals.

Method:

1. Collecting Urine:

Urine is a valuable source of nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. Collect urine separately to avoid contamination with other substances.

2. Preparing Wood Ash:

  • Burn untreated wood (hardwoods like oak, maple) to produce wood ash.
  • Allow the ash to cool and then sift out any large particles.
  • Store the wood ash in a dry, covered container.

3. Mixing Ingredients:

Wear appropriate safety gear like gloves and goggles while handling chemicals. In a well-ventilated area, mix 1 part urine, 1 part Epsom salt, and 1 part wood ash. You can adjust the ratios slightly based on the specific nutrient needs of your plants.

4. Aging the Blend:

Place the mixture in a sealed container and let it age for a few weeks. This allows the components to break down and blend together.

5. Application:

  • Before applying the fertilizer, dilute the mixture with water. A general ratio could be 1 part fertilizer mix to 10 parts water.
  • Apply the diluted mixture to the soil around your plants, avoiding direct contact with the foliage.

Note:

  • Conduct a soil test before using the fertilizer to ensure you are not over-applying nutrients.
  • Different plants have varying nutrient requirements, so adjust the application rates accordingly.
  • This project is a basic guideline. For precise nutrient ratios and plant-specific blends, consult agricultural extension services or experts in the field.

Project 2

Bio-Augmented Aging of Urine for Enhanced Fertilizer Production

Materials:

  • Urine Collection Containers: Clean containers for collecting human urine.
  • Storage Vessels: Airtight containers or barrels for aging urine.
  • pH Testing Kit: To monitor changes in urine acidity.
  • Time-Tracking Tools: Calendar or timer for monitoring aging duration.
  • Ventilation System: Optional for reducing ammonia odors during aging.
  • Bio Materials:
    • Compost or aged organic matter
    • Microbial inoculants (compost tea, effective microorganisms)

Methods:

  1. Urine Collection:
    • Collect fresh human urine in clean containers.
    • Ensure the urine is free from contaminants like soap or cleaning agents.
  2. Storage Setup:
    • Mix urine with compost or aged organic matter in the storage vessels to introduce beneficial microorganisms.
    • Transfer the mixture to airtight containers for aging.
  3. Aging Process:
    • Allow the urine and bio-material mixture to age for a specific duration (e.g., 4-8 weeks).
    • Monitor pH levels regularly to ensure a stable environment for nutrient transformation.
  4. Ventilation (Optional):
    • If odor is a concern, implement a ventilation system to release gases without losing essential nutrients.
  5. Microbial Inoculation:
    • Introduce microbial inoculants (compost tea, effective microorganisms) to enhance the breakdown of organic matter and nutrient release.
  6. pH Adjustment (Optional):
    • Adjust pH levels if necessary, aiming for a range suitable for plant absorption.
  7. Quality Assessment:
    • Periodically test aged urine for nutrient concentrations (nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium).

      Project Plan:

      1. Research Objectives:

        • Investigate the impact of added bio-materials on nutrient availability.

      2. Application Trials:

        • Conduct controlled experiments, comparing plant growth using bio-augmented aged urine vs. traditional aged urine.
        • Measure plant height, leaf size, and overall health as indicators of fertilizer effectiveness.

      3. Documentation:

        • Record changes in pH, nutrient levels, and plant growth throughout the aging process.
        • Document any challenges faced during the project and proposed solutions.

      4. Safety Measures:

        • Emphasize safety precautions, including proper hygiene during urine collection and protection against potential pathogens.

      Description:

      This project explores the synergistic effects of bio-augmented aging on urine, incorporating compost or aged organic matter and microbial inoculants. By introducing beneficial microorganisms, we aim to accelerate the breakdown of organic matter, enhance nutrient release, and create a more potent and sustainable urine-based fertilizer. The project includes comprehensive assessments of nutrient concentrations and controlled experiments to evaluate the impact on plant growth. Through careful documentation, we seek to provide insights into a holistic approach for optimizing the potential of aged urine as a nutrient-rich and eco-friendly fertilizer.

Fertilizer Components:

  • One bag of urea (20kg)
  • One bag of DAP (20kg)
  • Phosphate fertilizer bag (10kg)
  • 10 kg of first and second round fertilizer (basal and top dressing)
  • 5 liters of water
  • Plastic sheet or tarp for ground cover
  • Shovel
  • Hoe
  • Large sack or tarp for mixing the components

Fertilizer Components Usage:

  • Urea (Bran): Add essential nutrients like phosphorus and carbohydrates to improve crop growth.
  • DAP (Dung):
    • Supply necessary nutrients, especially nitrogen for poultry manure.
    • Use organic materials to support overall fertilizer effectiveness.
  • Phosphate Fertilizer (wood ash):
    • Add essential nutrients.
    • Use safe materials to prevent fertilizer burn.
    • Increase nutrient absorption in our soil.
  • Fertilizer: Apply necessary nutrients such as nitrogen. Assist in enhancing overall fertilizer effectiveness and accelerating crop growth.
  • Water: Provide moisture to facilitate the activation of applied fertilizers.

Application Guidelines:

  • Spread a plastic sheet on the ground and place the sack on it.
  • If applying to plants, place the plastic sheet underneath and fold it when done.

Application Process:

  • Choose suitable poultry or cattle manure, ensuring it's well-composted.
  • Spread one bag of urea on the prepared sheet.
  • Add one bag of DAP and mix thoroughly.
  • Incorporate the phosphate fertilizer and mix well.
  • Include 10 kg of the chosen basal or top dressing fertilizer.
  • Pour 5 liters of water, distributing it evenly.
  • Once the mixture is complete, fold the sheet over and mix thoroughly.
  • Apply the fertilizer to the field, and within 21 days, positive results should be visible.

Crop Planting:

  • For maize, plant seeds at a depth of 10 cm, spacing them 10 cm apart.
  • Water the field immediately after planting.

Harvesting:

  • These guidelines, along with proper hand cultivation and maintaining 260 plant stands per two square meters, ensure a bountiful harvest.

Local Recommendations for DIY Fertiliser

  • First stage: Apply fertilizer carefully.
  • Second stage: All fertilizers should be in one place.
  • Third stage: Mix the specified components.
  • Fourth stage: Apply the final touch.

Harvesting Tips:

  • When plucking, pick from the base and avoid breaking the branches.
  • Ensure the harvested fruits are mature and firm.
